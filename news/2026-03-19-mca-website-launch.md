It's here.

After a long time of the MCA existing entirely through Discord messages, pinned posts, and word of mouth, we now have a proper home on the internet. The official **Minecraft Club of America website** is live, and I have to say — it looks fantastic.

## What's on the Site

The site has everything you'd want to know about the MCA in one place.

The **Home page** gives you the full picture: who we are, what we do, our mission, our stats, and a gallery of screenshots from the Global Dawn server. If you've ever tried to explain the MCA to someone who's never heard of it, just send them the link.

The **Server page** has the Global Dawn server status — live, updating every 30 seconds — so you can check if the server is up before launching Minecraft. The address is kept private for security, but Discord members know where to find it.

The **Leadership page** lays out the full government structure: the Administrative Branch, the World Congress, and the World Court. All the cabinet positions, checks and balances, and recent legislation are documented there.

The **History page** covers the full journey from December 2021 all the way to Global Dawn — every server, every era, every key figure who helped build what the MCA is today.

And of course, there's the **News section** — which you're currently reading. Multiple MCA publications will be able to post here. If you're interested in contributing, see the pinned guidelines in the #news-professionals channel.

## A New Era

The website is more than just a nice-looking page. It's a signal that the MCA is growing into something real — an organization with a constitution, a government, a history, and now a public face.

[pullquote]We started as a handful of kids on a Minecraft server in Minot, North Dakota. Now we have a national organization, 60+ members, and a website. — William Rector, National President[/pullquote]

We're expanding. More clubs are on track for 2026. The goals are big, and we're building the infrastructure to reach them.

The website is one piece of that. It's the front door.
